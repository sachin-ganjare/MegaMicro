How to solve the problem with the USB port in Pendrive

If you're having issues with a USB port not recognizing or working properly with your pendrive (USB flash drive), you can follow these steps to troubleshoot and resolve the problem:

1.Try a Different USB Port: Plug your pendrive into a different USB port on your computer. This helps identify whether the issue is specific to the port or the pendrive.

2.Use Another Computer: Connect the pendrive to another computer. This helps determine if the problem is related to the pendrive itself or the computer's USB ports.

3. Check for Physical Damage: Inspect the USB port on your computer and the connectors on the pendrive for any physical damage. Bent pins or damaged connectors can cause connection issues.

4. Restart Your Computer: Sometimes, a simple restart can resolve USB port issues. Restart your computer and try connecting the pendrive again.

5. Update USB Drivers: Ensure that your USB drivers are up to date. Outdated drivers can lead to compatibility issues. Check the manufacturer's website for your computer or motherboard for the latest USB drivers.

6.Check Device Manager (Windows): In Windows, open Device Manager and look for any yellow exclamation marks or error messages related to USB devices. Right-click on the problematic device and select "Update driver" or "Uninstall device," then restart your computer.

6. Use a Different Cable: If you are using an external USB hub or extension cable, try connecting the pendrive directly to a USB port without using the hub or cable. Faulty cables or hubs can cause connection problems.

7.Check for Windows Updates: Ensure that your operating system is up to date. Windows updates may include fixes for USB-related issues.

8.Disable USB Selective Suspend (Windows): In the Power Options menu on Windows, disable USB selective suspend. This feature may cause USB devices to stop working to save power. Open the Power Options menu. Click on "Change plan settings." Click on "Change advanced power settings." Expand the "USB settings" and set "USB selective suspend setting" to "Disabled."

9. Inspect for Software Conflicts: Some software applications or drivers may conflict with USB devices. Try connecting the pendrive in Safe Mode to see if it works without interference.
